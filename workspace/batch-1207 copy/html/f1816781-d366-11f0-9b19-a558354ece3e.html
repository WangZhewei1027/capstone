What is Radix Sort?

Radix Sort is a non-comparative sorting algorithm that sorts numbers by processing individual digits. It works by:

Step 1: Find the maximum number to know the number of digits
Step 2: Sort numbers based on the least significant digit (LSD) or most significant digit (MSD)
Step 3: Continue sorting by the next significant digit until all digits are processed
Step 4: The numbers are now sorted in ascending order

How it Works

Radix sort uses a stable sorting algorithm (usually counting sort) as a subroutine to sort the digits. It processes digits from the least significant to the most significant (LSD radix sort) or vice versa (MSD radix sort).

For each digit position, numbers are distributed into "buckets" based on the digit value (0-9). After distribution, numbers are collected from the buckets in order, and the process repeats for the next digit position.

Time Complexity

O(d*(n+b))

Where d is the number of digits, n is the number of elements, and b is the base (10 for decimal)

Space Complexity

O(n+b)

Additional space for the buckets is required
